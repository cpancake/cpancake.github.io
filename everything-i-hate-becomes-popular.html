Everything I Hate Becomes Popular

Thu 14 April 2016
by Andrew Rogers

Sometimes, I create quick and dirty projects on a whim and then release them. And, for some reason, those seem to become more popular than things I put effort into.

My most popular GitHub repository is a one day project to build a Steam login system for Node.js. I'm not even sure it works anymore.

I created a joke flash game named "YouTube Let's Player Simulator 2014" to make fun of the state of video game LPs. It took three hours to make. The game currently has 181,377 plays.

My second most popular GitHub repository is a DeviantArt clone I attempted to make over four years ago. I'm not even sure there's any functionality there.

The three most popular YouTube videos on my channel are zero effort videos for a group of people I no longer want any part of.

Once, out of spite for a guy who really annoyed me, I fixed up the Garry's Mod gamemode Pirate Ship Wars to work with Garry's Mod 13. The original fix took a few hours. The last commit I made was three days later. The workshop item has 235,086 subscribers.

I feel like there must be a way to use this phenomenon to my advantage.

(This is not a humblebrag, I promise!)
